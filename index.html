Course Overview

Holistic Education

Our Bachelor of Science in Criminology program integrates theoretical knowledge with practical skills, focusing on the scientific study of crime, criminal behavior, and the justice system. We aim to develop critical thinkers and ethical professionals.

Career Opportunities

Graduates are prepared for diverse roles in law enforcement agencies (PNP, NBI, BJMP, BFP), correctional services, private security, forensics, and legal professions. Our alumni are highly sought after in the public and private sectors.

Mission and Vision

The Criminology Department envisions producing competent, compassionate, and community-oriented criminologists who uphold justice, promote peace and order, and contribute to national development. Our mission is to provide quality education and foster a culture of research and service.

Subject Highlights

The primary aim of Criminology 1 is to provide students with a foundational understanding of crime as a social phenomenon. It encourages critical thinking about the causes of crime, the effectiveness of various crime control strategies, and the complex relationship between crime, individuals, and society. This course is crucial for anyone considering a career in law enforcement, corrections, social work, legal professions, or any field related to public safety and justice.

This course provides a comprehensive overview of the five pillars of the Philippine Criminal Justice System: Law Enforcement, Prosecution, Courts, Corrections, and Community. Students will learn about the roles, functions, and interrelationships of these components, as well as the challenges and reforms within the system.

Focuses on the principles of police organization and management, including personnel management, police operations, intelligence gathering, and public relations. It covers the structures and functions of various law enforcement agencies in the Philippines and abroad.

This course introduces the fundamental principles and techniques of criminal investigation. Topics include crime scene processing, evidence collection and preservation, interviewing and interrogation, surveillance, and the role of intelligence in solving crimes.

Explores the concept of human rights within the context of criminal justice. It emphasizes the importance of human dignity and due process, examining international and national human rights instruments and their implications for law enforcement, corrections, and legal practice.

Delves into various criminological theories explaining why individuals commit crimes. It covers classical, positivist, sociological, psychological, and modern theories of crime, providing students with analytical frameworks to understand criminal behavior.

Introduces students to the essential information technology tools and applications used in criminal justice and public safety operations. Topics include data management systems, forensic software, cybersecurity basics, and digital evidence handling.
